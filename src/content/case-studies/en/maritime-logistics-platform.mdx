## Context

This product supported operational logistics work rather than generic admin flows. The frontend had to handle route planning, reporting, planned-vs-actual discrepancy visibility, and map-based vessel views in a way that stayed usable under real business pressure.

## My Role

- served as frontend team lead
- had no higher frontend layer above me
- coordinated directly with a PM, a business representative, and the backend lead
- led a team of three frontend engineers

## What I Owned

- frontend architecture decisions
- delivery coordination across the frontend stream
- collaboration around API contracts with the backend side
- interview participation and frontend team formation

## Product Work

- route planning views
- reporting interfaces
- planned-versus-actual discrepancy surfaces
- vessel display on maps

## Outcome

The system was used by multiple departments across different offices, so the frontend had to support operational clarity rather than just visual polish. The strongest part of this work was combining technical leadership with business-facing delivery in a product where the interfaces mattered to day-to-day operations.
